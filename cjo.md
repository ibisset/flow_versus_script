### Post-Conditional Job Offer (CJO) Process for NSA Summer STEM Internship

Congratulations on your CJO! This is a major milestone for the NSA Summer STEM Internship (typically a 12-week program running mid-May to mid-August). The CJO is **conditional** on completing the **suitability process**, which verifies your eligibility for a Top Secret/Sensitive Compartmented Information (TS/SCI) clearance with a full-scope polygraph. This process is sponsored by NSA and handled by the Defense Counterintelligence and Security Agency (DCSA) and NSA's security team. It's thorough but standard for all IC roles—expect 3–6 months total, with most interns clearing in time for their start date.

The process focuses on background checks, interviews, and assessments to ensure trustworthiness. Failure at any step can rescind the offer, but honesty and a clean history lead to success for ~90%+ of selected interns. Below is the step-by-step timeline based on official NSA guidance and recent applicant experiences (2023–2025 cycles). Timelines vary by your background (e.g., foreign travel or relatives can extend it).

#### Step-by-Step Process
| Step | Description | Timeline (from CJO) | What to Expect / Tips |
|------|-------------|----------------------|-----------------------|
| **1. Accept CJO & Submit Pre-Employment Paperwork** | Formally accept the offer via email or the IC Candidate Portal. Complete/update your SF-86 (Questionnaire for National Security Positions) and any supplemental forms (e.g., financial disclosures, release agreements). | Immediate (1–7 days) | Log into the portal at intelligencecareers.gov or via your recruiter. Be exhaustive—disclose everything (e.g., foreign contacts, drug history). Errors here flag later steps. If you have prior clearances, note them to expedite. |
| **2. Background Investigation Initiation** | NSA submits your SF-86 to DCSA for a Single Scope Background Investigation (SSBI). This includes records checks (criminal, credit, employment, education) and interviews with references, employers, neighbors, etc. | Starts 1–4 weeks after SF-86 | You'll get an email from CESP (Centralized e-Suitability Processing) confirming submission. Investigators may contact you for clarification (e.g., "Explain this 2019 traffic ticket"). Respond promptly. No travel abroad during this phase. |
| **3. Investigator Interview (if needed)** | A DCSA investigator conducts a 2–4 hour in-person or virtual interview to verify your SF-86. | 1–3 months after submission | Scheduled via email; prepare ID and any docs. It's conversational but detailed—expect questions on finances, relationships, and loyalties. Recent timelines: 2–4 months post-SF-86. |
| **4. Psychological Evaluation** | In-person (usually Fort Meade) assessment with a psychologist: 45–90 min of written tests (personality/integrity inventories) + 60–120 min clinical interview. | 1–4 months after CJO (often concurrent with BI) | Focuses on stability/resilience, not diagnosis. Disclose mental health history/meds honestly—treated conditions rarely disqualify. Lasts 1 day; results feed into adjudication. |
| **5. Full-Scope Polygraph Examination** | In-person at Fort Meade: 4–6 hour session covering lifestyle (drugs, finances, sex) and counterintelligence (foreign ties, leaks). May include 1–3 retakes for clarification. | 2–5 months after CJO | No "pass/fail" on first try—it's investigative. Practice calm breathing; be truthful. Most interns pass after 1–2 sessions. Scheduled after psych eval. |
| **6. Adjudication & Interim Clearance** | NSA's adjudication office reviews everything. If favorable, you get an interim TS/SCI (allows start date work) while full clearance finalizes. | 3–6 months total | ~80% get interim by start date. Full clearance can take 6–12 months post-start. If issues arise, you may need more docs or appeals. |
| **7. Final Job Offer (FJO) & Onboarding** | Recruiter issues FJO with salary (based on education: ~$8K–$12K for undergrad summer, prorated) and logistics (housing subsidy, travel). Attend orientation. | Upon interim clearance (or start date) | Sign digitally. Relocation: NSA covers flights; housing ~$1,500–2,500/mo (subsidized). Start: Mid-May; report to Fort Meade. |

#### Key Tips
- **Stay Responsive**: Check email/portal daily—delays from unanswered queries are common.
- **Avoid Red Flags**: No new tattoos/piercings, foreign travel, or financial changes. If on meds (e.g., for anxiety), get a doctor's note if severe history.
- **Hiring Freeze Note**: As of late 2024, a freeze affected some CJOs, but STEM internships are prioritized—yours should proceed unless notified.
- **Resources**: Contact your recruiter or summerinterns@uwe.nsa.gov. Track status in the IC Portal. For peer insights, check r/nsa or ClearanceJobs forums.
- **What If Delayed?**: NSA often grants start-date extensions or remote onboarding if interim lags.
